The Project

Nevada Infrastructure for Climate Change Science, Education, and Outreach

The goal of this project is to:

  • Create a statewide interdisciplinary program and virtual climate change center to....
  • Stimulate transformative research, education, and outreach on the effects of regional climate change on ecosystem services (especially water resources) and....
  • Support use of this knowledge by policy makers and stakeholders

It will create new interdisciplinary capability to detect, analyze, and model effects of regional climate change on landscapes, ecosystems, and water resources and communicate research results to decision makers and the public. Specific capabilities that are being developed include:

  • Regional climate modeling capability
  • Observational Network for Ecohydrology
  • Climate Change Data Portal
  • Enhanced Computational Capabilities (e.g., clusters, visualization)
  • New faculty
  • Graduate student support
  • Educational opportunities
  • Greater public awareness of climate change issues

Easily-distributed symmary information about the project is also available via:

  • The Nevada Climate Change Fact Sheet
  • The Nevada Climate Change Tri-Fold Brochure

The project is funded by a 5-year grant (2008-2013) from the National Science Foundation EPSCoR program (Experimental Program to Stimulate Competitive Research), under cooperative agreement EPS 0814372: Nevada Infrastructure for Climate Change Science, Education, and Outreach.
