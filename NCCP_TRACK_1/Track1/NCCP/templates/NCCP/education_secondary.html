Post Secondary Climate Change Science Activities

The NSF EPSCoR Nevada Climate Change project funded in 2012 the development of a number of college-level courses or course modules on climate change. These included courses in the social and natural sciences, English composition, and a course module in mathematics. Many of the courses were designed to be offered on-line. The courses/modules have or will be offered at specific institutions in Nevada. The course materials are being made publically available to encourage faculty at other institutions to offer similar courses on climate change. (Potential instructors should contact the original developers for exams and other assessment materials.)

The courses on this page include:

  • Sociology 4xx/6xx: The Social Dilemmas of Climate Change (developed at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, by Drs. Robert Futrell, Shannon Monnat, and Christie Batson).
  • Political Science 403L/603L: Climate Change Mitigation and Adaptation Policy (developed at the University of Nevada, Reno, by Dr. Derek Kauneckis).
  • English Composition II: Focus on Climate Change and Sustainability (developed at Great Basin College by Dr. Susanne Bentley).
  • Geography/Policy. GEOG 422/622 Climate Solutions: Local to Global Perspectives (developed at the University of Nevada, Reno, by Dr. Thomas Albright).
  • Geography/Policy. GEOG 280 Geospatial Atlas of Climate Change: Mapping Coupled Human-Environmental Aspects of Climate Change Impacting Nevada (developed at the University of Nevada, Reno, by Drs. Scott Nowicki and Gabriel Judkins).
  • Geography/Communications: GEOG 220X Interpreting Climate Science (developed at the Public Lands Institute, University of Nevada, Las Vegas, by Dr. Elizabeth Barrie).
  • Mathematical Modeling: A module for a capstone mathematical modeling course MATH 420/620 (developed at the University of Nevada, Reno, by Drs. Christopher Herald, Satoko Kurita, and Aleksey Telyakovskiy).
